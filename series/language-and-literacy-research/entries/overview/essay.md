# Sutton SignWriting Language and Literacy Research

*A research-facing series*

This series is the research-facing lane of the Sutton SignWriting platform.

Its purpose is to carry the more disciplined research-facing work that should not be forced into the public foundations lane or the support-facing infrastructure lane.

It is for:

- psycholinguists
- cognitive scientists
- literacy researchers
- language researchers
- sign-language researchers with developmental or experimental interests

The central questions of the series include:

- how language development depends on accessible pathways for input and expression
- how literacy functions as a second developmental layer rather than primary language creation
- how the four pathway model should be stated, tested, and refined
- what signed-language writing changes for development, reading, recognition, syntax, and research design
- what early studies, materials, corpora, and research assets should be built next

In this series, `pathway` is an access-and-development term.

It does not mean that each pathway maps to a separate neural circuit.

The series also distinguishes two literacy questions that are often blurred:

- how Deaf signers develop literacy in surrounding written languages such as Portuguese, English, or Korean
- what changes when a signed language itself also has durable readable and writable text

Both questions matter.

This series focuses mainly on the second while treating the first as essential rather than obsolete.

## What is here now

This series now has three research-facing anchors:

- *The Four Language Pathways*
- *Reading Signed Text*
- *Research Program for Literacy Without Sound*

That is intentional.

The lane begins with a clear model paper, moves into the reading problem, and then turns those questions into a buildable research program.

## Research posture

This series is not only a more technical version of `Sutton SignWriting Foundations`.

It is the place where the platform has to meet research-facing expectations:

- named engagement with language acquisition, sign-language acquisition, reading science, Deaf literacy, and corpus research
- explicit model statements and testable predictions where a paper proposes a model
- clear separation between conceptual claims, documented use, pilot observations, and controlled evidence
- ethics and governance language for work involving Deaf signers, learners, institutions, and reusable texts
- external-validity limits when Brazil-centered work is used as the strongest practical starting point

The series therefore uses `Sutton SignWriting Foundations` as the public conceptual opening, but it should not depend on that lane for its research apparatus.

## Current anchor

**The Four Language Pathways**

Current role:

- first true model paper of the lane
- derived in part from the ideas behind *Language Without Sound*
- more explicit, model-forward, and evidence-disciplined than the foundations series
- establishes the research-facing distinction between language pathways and literacy pathways

Most immediate continuation:

- *Reading Signed Text* as the next research-facing paper on recognition, syntax, spatial reference, and literacy questions

**Reading Signed Text**

Current role:

- second research-facing paper of the lane
- clarifies what reading would mean in a signed writing system
- turns the pathway model into a more explicit agenda around recognition, syntax, spatial reference, and reusable text

Most immediate continuation:

- *Research Program for Literacy Without Sound* as the bridge into actual study design and infrastructure needs

**Research Program for Literacy Without Sound**

Current role:

- third research-facing anchor of the lane
- turns the pathway model and reading agenda into study design
- defines early research lanes around reading and literacy, Brazil-grounded empirical work, and reusable corpora/materials
- keeps infrastructure visible as research infrastructure rather than turning the paper into a support brief

## Relationship to other series

- `Sutton SignWriting Foundations` remains the readable public conceptual lane.
- `Sutton SignWriting Language and Literacy Research` carries the more explicit developmental and literacy model.
- `Sutton SignWriting Evidence and Adoption` supports this series by showing the space is real.
- `Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure and Program` supports this series by explaining the stewardship and support structure needed for cumulative research.

Most immediate reader path:

- *Language Without Sound* -> *The Four Language Pathways* -> *Reading Signed Text* -> *Research Program for Literacy Without Sound*

`Sutton SignWriting Language and Literacy Research` is the current destination for the research-facing lane.
