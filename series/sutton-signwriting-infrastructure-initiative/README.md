# Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure Initiative

*A presentation and handout series for infrastructure, partnership, and sustainability framing*

This series is for materials that explain Sutton SignWriting as shared infrastructure, especially for institutional collaboration, international partnership, and long-term sustainability.

It should help a reader answer four questions quickly:

- what already exists
- what is currently fragile
- what kind of support structure makes sense
- which presentation should be used for which audience

## How To Use This Series

This series is not one long repeated argument.

It is a small deck set with different jobs.

Use the entries this way:

### 1. Executive Brief

Use this first when:

- someone needs a short forwardable summary
- a PI or administrator needs the fast version
- you want the cleanest "this already exists and needs support" framing

Main job:

- establish the infrastructure framing quickly
- show that the asset base already exists
- clarify that the missing layer is durable institutional support

### 2. Initiative Overview

Use this when:

- someone needs the general case for the initiative
- you want to explain the research and collaboration opportunity
- the audience needs the broad mission before the technical depth

Main job:

- frame SignWriting as globally used but still fragile infrastructure
- show why a PI or academic home matters
- introduce grants, collaboration, and Platform V3 in a balanced way

### 3. Infrastructure and Stewardship

Use this when:

- the audience needs more authority and specificity
- a PI, lab, or institutional partner wants to know what assets actually exist
- you need to move beyond general concern into concrete stewardship reality

Main job:

- make the domains, servers, code, data, and standards visible
- show that there is already active stewardship and continuity responsibility
- explain why the real task is stabilization, documentation, and institutionalization

### 4. International Shared Support

Use this when:

- the conversation is about partnership beyond one institution
- international collaborators need to see how they fit
- the audience is thinking about support letters, shared participation, or global continuity

Main job:

- explain the international support model
- connect University of Iowa anchoring with cross-country participation
- show why global use strengthens the institutional case

### 5. Country Ambassadors

Use this when:

- the discussion becomes operational at the country level
- local outreach, translation, or relationship-building needs to be explained
- someone wants to understand the volunteer-first international model

Main job:

- define the ambassador role clearly
- keep authority boundaries disciplined
- show how local relationship capacity can grow without overbuilding too early

## Current Entries

- **Executive Brief** — forwardable PI-friendly summary of Sutton SignWriting as research infrastructure
- **Initiative Overview** — research infrastructure and collaboration opportunity
- **Infrastructure and Stewardship** — concrete asset inventory, continuity, and institutional stabilization case
- **International Shared Support** — international partnership and country-ambassador framing
- **Country Ambassadors** — volunteer-first country-level outreach and relationship-building model

## Series role

This series should carry the outward-facing infrastructure case:

- why the work matters
- why the current maintenance model is fragile
- what institutional and partner support makes possible
- how SignWriting Platform V3 fits the long-term direction

## Reading Order

For most institutional conversations, the recommended order is:

1. **Executive Brief**
2. **Initiative Overview**
3. **Infrastructure and Stewardship**
4. **International Shared Support**
5. **Country Ambassadors**

That order moves from:

- quick framing
- to general initiative case
- to authoritative stewardship depth
- to international partnership structure
- to country-level operational support
