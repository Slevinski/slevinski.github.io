# Brazil Key Works

**A compact bibliography for Brazil as the empirical center of SignWriting**

This note is intentionally selective.

It is not a full bibliography of SignWriting in Brazil.

It is a compact list of works and public records that give the Brazil brief a stronger scholarly spine.

## 1. Core historical and institutional anchors

These are the shortest path into the documented public record.

**Project record | [Meta-Wiki. (2018). *Brazilian Sign Language Wikipedia Project*](https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Grants:Project/slevinski/Brazilian_Sign_Language_Wikipedia_Project)**  
Best compact documented floor: 21 schools/universities, 13 states, 1000+ students.

**Country archive | [SignWriting.org. (n.d.). *SignWriting in Brazil*](https://www.signwriting.org/brazil/)**  
Public country hub linking historical, educational, and software-related materials.

**Early history | [SignWriting.org. (n.d.). *Brazil uses SignWriting: 1996-1997*](https://www.signwriting.org/brazil/brazil07.html)**  
Useful for early timeline and national historical framing.

**Symposium anchor | [SignWriting Symposium. (2014). *SignWriting in Brazilian Deaf Education: 1996 to present*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0024.html)**  
Best single public presentation for historical and institutional overview.

## 2. Foundational academic works

These works help show that Brazil is not only a use case but a research center.

**Dissertation | [Stumpf, M. R. (2005). *Aprendizagem de escrita de língua de sinais pelo sistema SignWriting*](https://lume.ufrgs.br/handle/10183/5429)**  
Foundational Brazilian doctoral work on learning written sign in paper and computer contexts.

**Dissertation | [Bózoli, D. M. F. (2021). *Educação bilíngue de surdos: o uso da escrita de sinais SignWriting na aprendizagem do português como segunda língua*](https://repositorio.ufsc.br/handle/123456789/227006)**  
Strong bridge to bilingual education and SignWriting in current Brazilian Deaf education.

**Archive / dissertation ecosystem | SignWriting research archives and Brazilian dissertation trails**  
Shows that the Brazilian record extends beyond isolated theses.

## 3. Symposium works on literacy, reading, and education

These help show what Brazilian researchers and educators are publicly emphasizing.

**2014 | [*Aspects of reading and SignWriting*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0025.html)**  
Strong early public reading-focused presentation.

**2014 | [*Teaching and learning SignWriting in an online course*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0026.html)**  
Documents distance-learning scale and pedagogy.

**2014 | [*Magic behind the cams: Teaching SignWriting in an online course*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0027.html)**  
Shows the media and instructional labor behind online SignWriting teaching.

**2014 | [*The perspective of the Deaf from Ipiaú, Bahia, Brazil about SignWriting*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0018.html)**  
Useful community and learner perspective.

**2017 | [*Sign Language Writing: SignWriting as a Tool in Deaf Literacy*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0068.html)**  
Explicit literacy framing in Brazilian public presentation form.

**2024 | [*Implementação do SignWriting em currículos escolares bilíngues*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0078.html)**  
Strong recent curricular framing.

**2024 | [*Leitura de textos em Libras (SignWriting) por Surdos acadêmicos: conforto linguístico e identificação cultural*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0092.html)**  
Strong recent evidence that the Brazil record now includes reader-comfort and cultural-identification questions.

## 4. Software, corpus, and technical works

These matter because the Brazil case is not only pedagogical.

**2015 | [*SignWriting Interface for a Journal System*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0048.html)**  
Shows Brazilian interest in scholarly and publishing infrastructure.

**2016 | [*A Web Tool for Building Parallel Corpora of Spoken and Sign Languages*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0057.html)**  
Helps connect Brazil to corpus and computational infrastructure.

**2024 | [*Uma análise fonológica para a escrita em SignWriting do léxico CASA, através da plataforma SignPuddle*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0093.html)**  
Combines lexical analysis, phonology, and platform-based corpus use.

## 5. Books and teaching materials

These help show script culture, not just academic argument.

**Book / presentation trail | [*Escrita de Sinais sem Mistérios*](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0035.html)**  
Important Brazilian teaching-material line with repeated public visibility.

**Contemporary educational work | *Escrita de Sinais na Educação de Surdos***  
Important current candidate for translation, access, and educational-method discussion if permissions are secured.

**2024 symposium book and literature presentations | [books, poetry, and written Libras materials](https://www.signwriting.org/symposium/presentation0086.html)**  
Show that Brazilian SignWriting is moving beyond pedagogy into visible literary and cultural production.

## 6. Best short reading path

If someone needs the fastest path into the Brazil record, start here:

1. Meta-Wiki 2018 project accounting  
2. SignWriting in Brazilian Deaf Education: 1996 to present  
3. Stumpf dissertation  
4. Bózoli dissertation  
5. the 2024 reading-comfort and curriculum symposium presentations
