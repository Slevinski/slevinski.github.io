# Country Ambassadors

Purpose: define a lightweight international ambassador model to support outreach, communication, and institutional relationship-building in each country.

## Why This Role Is Needed

International outreach is difficult to centralize well.

Each country has different:

* university structures
* languages and communication norms
* funding realities and economic conditions
* SignWriting communities, leaders, and institutional pathways

A country ambassador model creates local relationship capacity without requiring the central team to understand every context alone.

## Role Summary

A **Country Ambassador** is a trusted local volunteer who helps connect the Sutton SignWriting initiative with institutions, educators, researchers, and community leaders in a specific country.

This begins as a **volunteer service role** with the expectation that, as funding becomes available, the role may evolve into a **paid stipend position for services rendered** and to support local SignWriting work.

## Core Goals

Country ambassadors help:

* identify universities and institutional contacts
* make outreach more culturally and linguistically appropriate
* explain local conditions, needs, and constraints
* build trust with educational and research partners
* gather letters of support, usage statements, and collaboration interest
* strengthen the case for grants, gifts, and donations through the University of Iowa

## Typical Responsibilities

Responsibilities may include:

* mapping relevant universities, schools, programs, and community organizations
* identifying faculty, administrators, and local champions
* helping translate or adapt outreach materials
* making introductions and supporting early conversations
* sharing local feedback about feasibility, priorities, and concerns
* documenting visible SignWriting use and impact in the country
* helping identify possible future collaborators, advisors, or donors

## What Ambassadors Are Not

Country ambassadors are not expected to:

* make legal or financial commitments on behalf of the project
* independently negotiate institutional agreements
* serve as payroll or payment intermediaries
* represent the University of Iowa in a formal administrative capacity unless specifically authorized

Their role is relational, informational, and community-building.

## Volunteer-First Model

The initial ambassador structure should be simple:

* start with volunteer appointments
* define country scope clearly
* agree on reasonable expected activities
* provide shared messaging and outreach materials
* recognize contributions visibly and respectfully

This keeps the barrier to entry low while building real international capacity.

## Path to Stipends

As institutional support grows, ambassadors may be eligible for modest stipends tied to clearly defined services such as:

* translation or localization work
* outreach coordination
* institutional engagement support
* event or meeting facilitation
* documentation of national use cases and partnerships

Stipends should be introduced only when administrative and funding pathways are clear.

Because economic conditions vary widely across countries, compensation should be handled thoughtfully rather than through a one-size-fits-all model.

## Selection Criteria

Strong ambassadors are likely to have some combination of:

* trusted standing in local SignWriting or Deaf education communities
* strong communication skills
* familiarity with local universities or institutional networks
* ability to work across language and cultural boundaries
* reliability in documenting conversations and follow-up

## Initial Countries

A practical first cohort could include:

* Brazil
* Mexico
* South Korea
* additional countries where active SignWriting use and institutional interest are already visible

## Support Needed From the Central Team

To make the model work, the central project should provide:

* a short ambassador description
* a simple onboarding note
* outreach templates that can be localized
* a shared contact and follow-up tracker
* clear explanation of how ambassador work connects to grants, gifts, donations, and partnerships

## Recommended Next Step

Recruit a small pilot group of ambassadors in 3 to 5 countries and test the model before expanding further.
