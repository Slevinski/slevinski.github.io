# **Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure Initiative**

**International Shared Support and Partnership Framework**

## **Overview**

Sutton SignWriting is a writing system for signed languages used in education, research, and communication. It supports literacy, accessibility, and direct written expression in signed languages.

The system is used internationally, with active relationships and visible impact in Brazil, Mexico, South Korea, and other regions. Students, teachers, researchers, and institutions depend on its tools, websites, and data resources.

---

## **Current Situation**

The digital infrastructure supporting SignWriting is currently maintained independently.

A recent outage affected educational use in Brazil and highlighted a broader issue:

> a globally used system is still relying on a fragile maintenance model.

The project now needs a more durable structure that combines institutional support, international collaboration, and a credible administrative home.

---

## **Proposed Support Model**

The preferred model is:

* **grants, gifts, and donations administered through the University of Iowa**
* **international partner relationships** that demonstrate impact, inform priorities, and strengthen proposals
* **country ambassadors** who can support local outreach across language, institutional, and economic differences
* **shared institutional participation** rather than dependence on a single independent maintainer

This is not only a Brazil support plan. It is an international infrastructure strategy with Brazil as one of the clearest and strongest examples of active use.

### Key Idea

> SignWriting should be sustained as shared linguistic and educational infrastructure with institutional backing and international participation.

---

## **What Support Enables**

Institutional and partner support will help:

* maintain and stabilize core SignWriting services
* improve uptime, recovery, and long-term preservation
* support continued development of tools and standards
* provide a reliable platform for students, teachers, researchers, and community programs
* strengthen the foundation for future research, collaboration, and educational deployments

---

## **Why International Participation Matters**

International relationships are important for more than outreach.

They help demonstrate:

* real-world educational reliance
* cross-country research value
* language-access and accessibility relevance
* readiness for multi-institution collaboration
* demand for stable stewardship over time

Brazil remains especially important because of active educational use, but the broader network across Mexico, South Korea, and other regions strengthens the case that this is global infrastructure rather than a local or isolated project.

---

## **How Partners Can Participate**

Partner institutions do not all need to contribute in the same way.

Possible forms of participation include:

* letters of support for grant proposals
* statements of educational or research use
* introductions to faculty, administrators, or development contacts
* collaboration on international proposals
* modest shared-support contributions where appropriate
* participation in future advisory or partner conversations

## **Country Ambassadors**

Because communication varies widely across countries, the project is also seeking **Country Ambassadors**.

These ambassadors would begin as volunteers who help with:

* identifying universities and local champions
* improving communication across language and cultural differences
* adapting outreach to local institutional structures
* supporting introductions, follow-up, and relationship-building

Over time, and as funding becomes available, this role may develop into a modest **stipend-supported service position** tied to outreach, translation, coordination, and other locally valuable work.

This model is intended to make international collaboration more practical, respectful, and sustainable.

---

## **University of Iowa Role**

The University of Iowa provides the strongest current home for this work because it can support:

* grant framing and institutional collaboration
* administration of gifts and donations
* research integration and PI alignment
* credibility for long-term stewardship and reporting

International partner relationships should support and strengthen this institutional lane, not replace it.

---

## **Current and Future Development**

* support for institutional and educational use
* anonymized demographic data for research
* improved tools for collaboration, access, and long-term platform sustainability
