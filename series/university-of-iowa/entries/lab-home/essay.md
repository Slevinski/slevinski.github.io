# Lab Home

**A practical case for placing Steve Slevinski in a PI's lab now**

## Core message

The first move is small, practical, and immediate:

- place Steve Slevinski in a PI's lab now
- begin at 0 percent salary support if necessary
- use that placement to create a real base for the next steps

The University of Iowa is not being asked to fund a fully developed center all at once.

It is asking for the first enabling structure:

> a lab home that makes the rest of the work possible

That one decision changes the environment around the work. It gives Steve an academic base, gives other collaborators a place to connect, and makes future grant writing, salary support, and infrastructure planning far more credible.

This document stays intentionally narrow.

Its job is to explain the PI decision clearly and keep the first step easy to understand.

## Why this matters now

The timing matters because the work already has practical value and real long-term potential, but it is harder to stabilize either one without a real home inside the University.

A lab home matters now because it:

- gives the work a place to live inside the University
- gives the work an academic and administrative location
- makes conversations with collaborators more concrete
- creates a place from which grant concepts can be developed
- gives advocates a clearer support target
- allows gifts or donations to be routed through a PI and institutional structure rather than through an improvised arrangement

Without that first structure, every later conversation becomes more difficult.

People may still be interested in the work, but the next step remains vague. A lab home turns a vague future into an actionable path.

## What 0 percent means

The phrase 0 percent can sound fragile or provisional if it is not explained clearly.

What it actually means here is:

- the placement comes first
- salary support can grow around the placement
- the first institutional decision is intentionally smaller than the full future vision

This is a reduced-friction model.

The immediate ask is disciplined:

- the work already has value
- support can grow around the placement
- the first institutional step remains smaller than the full future structure

The larger structure can then grow around that base through several reinforcing lanes:

- partial salary support
- project-based DMS and repository work
- grants under the PI
- gifts and donations as a bridge-support lane
- longer-term research infrastructure development

The strength of this model is that it does not require one conversation or one funding source to solve everything at once.

## What the PI is actually being asked to do

The PI is being asked to provide:

- a lab home
- academic affiliation and credibility
- a real university base for future work

This is important because it keeps the ask clear.

The ask is:

> provide the first real home so useful work can begin now and broader support can grow around it

## Why a PI benefits

The strongest version of this proposal is a practical value proposition for the PI and lab.

### Immediate benefit

Steve can help with current and near-term research needs, especially:

- Data Management and Sharing Plan development and revision
- workflow design across complex project systems
- repository preparation and submission support
- technical help in the space between local data systems and external delivery
- standards guidance where structured data and documentation are needed

This means the lab gains useful operational capacity rather than only taking on an additional administrative relationship.

The fuller operational case belongs in **Steve Slevinski Value**.

### Medium-term benefit

Once Steve is institutionally placed, the PI gains a stronger base for:

- cross-project DMS and repository support
- collaborative problem-solving around data workflows
- grant concept development
- additional faculty and project conversations
- building a broader set of collaborations around Steve's skills

### Long-term benefit

The lab also becomes the place from which a broader research infrastructure effort can grow.

That longer arc includes:

- Sutton SignWriting support
- software maintenance
- data and corpus infrastructure
- collaboration across literacy, language, accessibility, and research systems

The point is not that the PI must immediately own the whole future initiative.

The point is that the PI becomes the person who made the future possible by supporting the right first step.

## What Steve brings immediately

The case for a lab home is stronger because Steve already brings practical value now.

His near-term usefulness includes:

- shaping DMS plans into real workflows
- helping teams organize data from mixed systems
- supporting validation, naming, and repository preparation
- helping projects finish difficult delivery work under real deadlines

This is especially relevant for projects that use combinations of:

- REDCap
- spreadsheets and CSV files
- technical system outputs
- imaging-related data
- external collaborators or vendors
- repositories such as NDA

The practical value here is not theoretical. It is the kind of work that helps projects avoid confusion, reduce delay, and finish difficult repository and compliance tasks successfully.

## The support model

The long-term strength of this plan comes from having several reinforcing lanes instead of one all-or-nothing lane.

These lanes matter because placement does not have to carry the whole future by itself.

### Lane 1: Lab home

The first requirement is a lab home under a PI.

This is the anchor for everything else.

### Lane 2: Partial salary support

Advocates and collaborators may be able to help secure part of Steve's salary support.

This matters because it:

- lowers the immediate burden on any one source
- creates runway
- gives the arrangement more stability while broader support develops

### Lane 3: DMS and repository work

Steve's practical usefulness can create relationships across the University through:

- DMS planning
- workflow support
- repository delivery work
- technical collaboration around data organization and validation

That lane creates value before the larger SignWriting infrastructure vision needs to be fully developed.

### Lane 4: Grant writing under the PI

Once Steve is institutionally based, grants can be written under the PI to support:

- Steve's role
- project-facing DMS and repository work
- software and documentation maintenance
- broader Sutton SignWriting infrastructure
- future research collaboration

### Lane 5: Gifts and donations

There is also a complementary bridge-support lane through gifts and donations.

It can support:

- bridge support
- follow-through support
- infrastructure support

It works best as a disciplined complement to grants and salary support, not as a substitute for them.

## Why broader University relationships matter

The lab home is the anchor, but the model works best when Steve is also visible and useful beyond a single room or single project.

That matters for several reasons:

- some researchers need immediate DMS help now
- some teams need practical repository delivery support now
- some collaborators may become future co-investigators or advocates
- some units may be able to contribute salary support or project-based support
- broader usefulness increases the credibility of later grant language

The lab home is:

- the first relationship
- the administrative and academic base
- the structure that makes outward-facing collaboration easier

It should not be understood as the final limit of the work.

## Why this is low risk and high upside

This is a low-risk first step because:

- the immediate ask is small
- the practical value is already clear
- multiple support lanes can grow around the placement
- the lab gains useful capacity right away

It is high upside because:

- salary support becomes more plausible
- grant writing becomes more credible
- cross-campus collaboration becomes easier
- Sutton SignWriting infrastructure becomes structurally possible

This is exactly the kind of situation where a modest first institutional decision can unlock a much larger set of outcomes later.

The immediate ask is straightforward:

Everything else becomes easier once that first step is real.

## Closing

The main question is not whether the entire future infrastructure is already funded.

The main question is whether the University can support the right first structure now.

If there is a lab home now, then:

- the work has a real place to start
- salary support becomes easier to build
- DMS and repository collaborations become easier to activate
- grant writing becomes more credible
- the broader Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure Initiative becomes more realistic

That is why the lab home matters so much.

It is not the end goal.

It is the first move that allows the rest of the strategy to work.
