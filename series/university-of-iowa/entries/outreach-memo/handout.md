# University of Iowa Outreach Memo

**A concise summary of the full opportunity**

## Purpose

This memo is the short internal-facing summary for University of Iowa faculty, research staff, grants staff, development staff, and other stakeholders who need the whole opportunity explained quickly and clearly.

Its job is not to replace the other decks.

Its job is to:

- summarize the whole structure
- explain why the opportunity matters now
- show why Steve is useful immediately
- clarify the larger SignWriting infrastructure opportunity
- route people into the right next conversation

This should be the easiest deck to forward internally when someone needs the short version first.

## One-paragraph summary

The immediate opportunity is to secure a lab home under a PI so that Steve Slevinski can be institutionally based while creating immediate value through Data Management and Sharing Plan work, workflow support, code and data shaping, and repository delivery. That lab home then becomes the structure from which collaborators, partial salary support, grant writing, and the longer-term Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure Initiative can grow. Gifts and donations can serve as a complementary continuity lane through that same structure.

## Why now

This is timely because the work already matters, but the current structure is still too fragile.

The goal is not to fully solve everything in one move.

The goal is to take the right first step:

- secure a lab home
- create institutional continuity
- make future grants and collaborations credible

That first step changes what becomes possible next.

Without that first structure:

- collaborators have no stable point of contact
- grant language is harder to ground
- salary support conversations are harder to route
- the larger infrastructure story remains more abstract than it should

With it, the whole structure becomes clearer.

## Why Steve matters now

Steve Slevinski creates immediate operational value through:

- Data Management and Sharing Plan support
- workflow design across mixed project systems
- code and data shaping
- repository-facing technical work
- support for validation, harmonization, and submission

This is important because the case for supporting Steve is not only future-oriented.

It is already justified by practical value that can help active projects succeed now.

The strongest case is:

> Steve helps research teams move from fragmented systems and compliance expectations toward structured workflow execution and successful repository delivery.

That is useful to real projects immediately.

## Why the University of Iowa is a strong fit

The opportunity fits the University of Iowa because it connects:

- accessibility and language equity
- literacy and Deaf education
- linguistics and multimodal representation
- research systems and data workflows
- neuroscience and cognition pathways
- international collaboration

This is not a narrow one-field opportunity.

It touches several institutional strengths at once, which is one reason it can support several different kinds of internal conversation.

## The larger opportunity

Beyond immediate practical value, there is a larger mission.

Sutton SignWriting is already globally used infrastructure for signed language literacy, education, and research, but it remains structurally fragile.

With the right academic home, grant writing, collaboration, and stewardship, it can become durable shared research infrastructure rather than vulnerable independent maintenance.

This larger opportunity should be described carefully.

It is strongest when it follows the practical value story rather than replacing it.

The right order is:

1. Steve is useful now
2. a lab home gives that usefulness institutional structure
3. that structure makes grants and longer-term infrastructure credible

## What support could look like

The support model is layered rather than singular.

### 1. Lab home

The first requirement is a lab home under a PI.

This is the anchor.

### 2. Partial salary support

Advocates and collaborators may help secure some percentage of salary support.

This creates continuity and lowers the burden on any single source.

### 3. Grant writing

Once institutionally based, grants can be written under the PI to support:

- Steve's role
- DMS and repository work
- software and infrastructure stewardship
- broader Sutton SignWriting infrastructure

### 4. Gifts and donations

Gifts and donations can provide:

- continuity support
- bridge support
- infrastructure support

These should be treated as complementary to grants, not as the primary funding model.

## Why this layered model matters

This model matters because it avoids an all-or-nothing problem.

No single lane has to do everything immediately.

Instead:

- the lab home creates structure
- salary support adds continuity
- grants support scale and research credibility
- gifts and donations add flexibility and bridge support

That makes the whole opportunity more realistic and easier to discuss across different institutional roles.

## Recommended next conversations

The most useful next conversations now are with:

- a PI or faculty collaborator who can provide a lab home

- researchers and labs that need DMS and repository help
- grants staff who can help shape proposals

The memo is valuable because not everyone needs the same next step.

The memo should help each reader identify which lane they belong in.

## How this memo should be used

This memo works best as:

- a forwarding link for internal stakeholders
- a short entry point before a longer deck
- a bridge between practical support and long-term infrastructure discussion

It should not be overloaded with too much detail.
