# University of Iowa

*Iowa-facing materials for practical research support, PI or lab base conversations, and Sutton SignWriting infrastructure alignment*

The University of Iowa opportunity begins with practical value.

Steve Slevinski can help active research teams with the work that often sits between a formal requirement and a finished deliverable:

- Data Management and Sharing Plans that match the real project lifecycle
- workflow implementation across REDCap, spreadsheets, exports, scripts, data dictionaries, and documentation
- repository delivery for NDA and similar destinations
- technical follow-through when validation, harmonization, submission, or recurring updates become difficult

That near-term value is the strongest first reason to talk.

The longer opportunity is also real. Steve maintains and extends Sutton SignWriting technical infrastructure: software, standards, domains, data, public resources, and the formal model that makes signed-language writing usable in software and research. Iowa could become a stronger stewardship structure for that work over time, but the first case should stay grounded in practical research support that labs can use now.

## What Iowa Can Gain

Iowa can gain useful research capacity in three connected ways.

First, active labs can get help with DMS Plans, workflow implementation, and repository delivery.

Second, a PI or lab can provide an academic base that makes collaboration, grants, support, and accountability easier to organize.

Third, the same base can become a credible starting point for longer-term SignWriting infrastructure, publication, and research collaboration.

The point is not to ask every reader to understand the entire future at once. The point is to make the first useful step clear.

## Core Purpose

This series should help Iowa readers answer practical questions:

- What useful work can Steve do for research teams now?
- Why would a PI or lab benefit from providing an academic base?
- How can support begin without pretending the whole future is already funded?
- How do DMS Plan, workflow implementation, repository delivery, and SignWriting infrastructure connect?
- Which conversation should happen next?

## Reading Order

The strongest sequence is:

1. **Research Team Value** when the reader needs to understand immediate research-team value.
2. **PI or Lab Base** when the reader is a PI, faculty collaborator, or administrator considering an institutional base.
3. **Outreach Memo** for a quick internal summary or forwarding note.
4. **Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure and Program** when the conversation becomes about the general infrastructure, philanthropy, host, or precedent case.
