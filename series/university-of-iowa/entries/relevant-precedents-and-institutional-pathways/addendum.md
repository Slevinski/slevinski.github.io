# Relevant Precedents and Institutional Pathways Addendum

_Companion addendum for **Relevant Precedents and Institutional Pathways**_

Each example below plays a different role in the larger argument, and each precedent is strongest in a different place.

## What these precedents establish

These examples are not exact matches.

They are better understood as comparable institutional patterns.

The strongest framing is:

- SignWriting already has meaningful technical and public assets
- universities often provide durable homes to exactly this kind of already-real infrastructure
- practical shared capacity often comes before large-scale formalization
- grants and philanthropy usually work best once a credible home already exists

## What each example is doing for the argument

### ICPSR

ICPSR is the strongest precedent for:

- shared research-data infrastructure
- institutional stewardship of an already-real technical need
- long-term curation, documentation, and public researcher services

Use it when the conversation is about:

- why university housing matters
- why stewardship should not remain informal
- why a mature asset base can justify durable administrative structure

### ELDP / ELAR

ELDP and ELAR are strongest for:

- philanthropy as a launch mechanism
- pairing funding with preservation obligations
- institutional legitimacy for language infrastructure

Use them when the conversation is about:

- why philanthropy can help
- why philanthropy should reinforce structure rather than replace it
- how language documentation and archive work can scale when anchored properly

### PARADISEC

PARADISEC is strongest for:

- distributed institutional support
- preservation as real infrastructure
- public and community access as a core design principle

Use it when the conversation is about:

- consortial or multi-unit support
- long-term preservation obligations
- why access and archiving are not side issues

### BSL Corpus Project and BSL SignBank

This is the strongest sign-language-specific precedent for:

- corpus to lexical resource evolution
- university stewardship of sign-language infrastructure
- ongoing use across research, teaching, and community access

Use it when the conversation is about:

- why sign-language resources become infrastructure
- why public lexical tools and corpora matter
- why SignWriting should not be treated as a niche side project

### ASLLRP / ASL SignBank / ASLLVD

This example is especially helpful for:

- showing the natural shape of sign-language technical ecosystems
- reinforcing the value of corpus, lexical, and annotation infrastructure
- showing that sign-language resources often require long-term technical stewardship

### MIDAS

MIDAS is not a language archive example.

It is useful because it models:

- shared technical capacity
- proposal support
- pilot funding
- cross-campus infrastructure services

Use it when the conversation is about:

- what Steve could do immediately inside a lab or institute
- why proposal support and operational capacity should come first
- how a modest starting structure can create broader grant leverage

## Suggested framing language

The most defensible language is:

- a recognizable path
- comparable institutional pattern
- relevant precedent
- adjacent but useful analog

Avoid saying:

- exact model
- direct duplicate
- proof that SignWriting will necessarily scale the same way

## Caveats worth naming

These precedents differ from SignWriting in important ways:

- some are archives first, while SignWriting also includes standards, software, and writing-system stewardship
- some began with major philanthropic gifts, while SignWriting may begin with institutional placement and practical capacity
- some are consortial or institute-based rather than lab-based
- some focused on spoken-language documentation, while SignWriting includes written signed-language infrastructure

Naming these differences strengthens the argument rather than weakening it.

It shows the comparison is thoughtful and not overstated.

## Bottom line

This precedent set supports a disciplined claim:

SignWriting does not need a university to invent its value.

It needs a university to recognize, house, and scale value that already exists.
